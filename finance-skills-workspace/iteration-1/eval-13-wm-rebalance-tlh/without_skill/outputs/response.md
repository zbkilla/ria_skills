# Rebalancing and Tax-Loss Harvesting Analysis

## Current Situation

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Portfolio value | $800,000 |
| Target allocation | 60% stocks / 40% bonds |
| Current allocation | 68% stocks / 32% bonds |
| Equity overweight | +8 percentage points |
| Threshold band | +/- 5% |
| Unrealized losses | ~$15,000 in select equity positions |

## Should You Rebalance?

**Yes.** Your equity allocation at 68% exceeds the upper threshold of 65% (60% target + 5% band). You are 3 percentage points beyond your tolerance band, which triggers a rebalance under a threshold-based discipline.

### Dollar Amounts

- **Current stocks:** $800,000 x 68% = $544,000
- **Target stocks:** $800,000 x 60% = $480,000
- **Current bonds:** $800,000 x 32% = $256,000
- **Target bonds:** $800,000 x 40% = $320,000
- **Amount to shift from stocks to bonds:** approximately $64,000

## Coordinating Rebalancing with Tax-Loss Harvesting

Since this is a taxable account and you need to sell equities to rebalance, you have a valuable opportunity to coordinate the two strategies. Here is how to approach it:

### Step 1: Harvest the Losses First

Identify the specific equity positions with the $15,000 in unrealized losses. Sell those positions first as part of your rebalancing sales. This accomplishes two goals simultaneously:

- It reduces your equity allocation (rebalancing goal)
- It realizes $15,000 in capital losses (tax benefit)

Those harvested losses can offset capital gains dollar-for-dollar, and if losses exceed gains, up to $3,000 per year can offset ordinary income, with the remainder carrying forward.

### Step 2: Sell Additional Equity if Needed

After harvesting the loss positions, check whether the proceeds are sufficient to close the $64,000 gap. If the loss positions represent less than $64,000 in market value, you will need to sell additional equity holdings. When choosing which additional positions to sell:

- Prefer positions with the smallest embedded gains (lowest tax cost)
- Consider selling positions held longer than one year to benefit from long-term capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20%) rather than short-term rates (taxed as ordinary income)
- Use specific lot identification (not average cost) to control which tax lots are sold

### Step 3: Reinvest Proceeds into Bonds

Deploy the total sale proceeds (~$64,000) into your bond allocation to bring it from $256,000 up to the $320,000 target.

### Step 4: Avoid Wash Sale Violations

If you want to maintain similar equity exposure for the harvested-loss positions (for example, if you sold a specific stock or fund), you must avoid repurchasing a "substantially identical" security within 30 days before or after the sale. Strategies to maintain exposure while respecting the wash sale rule:

- **Substitute with a similar but not identical fund.** For example, if you sold an S&P 500 index fund, you could buy a total market index fund or a large-cap fund tracking a different index during the 30-day window.
- **Wait 31 days and repurchase.** This is simpler but leaves you temporarily underweight in that specific segment.
- **In this case, since you are rebalancing down in equities anyway**, you may not need to replace the sold equity positions at all. The rebalancing target calls for less equity exposure, so the wash sale concern is reduced or eliminated.

### Step 5: Net Tax Impact Estimate

The $15,000 in harvested losses will offset gains from the other equity sales needed to complete the rebalance. For example:

- If additional equity sales generate $10,000 in realized gains, the harvested losses fully offset them, leaving $5,000 in net losses to carry forward or offset other income.
- If no other gains are realized (e.g., the loss positions alone covered the $64,000 shift), the full $15,000 in losses is available to offset other gains or up to $3,000 of ordinary income annually.

## Summary of Recommended Actions

1. **Sell the equity positions carrying $15,000 in unrealized losses** -- this simultaneously rebalances and harvests tax losses.
2. **Sell additional equity positions as needed** to reach the full ~$64,000 reduction, prioritizing long-term holdings with minimal embedded gains.
3. **Invest all proceeds into bonds** to restore the 60/40 target.
4. **Avoid repurchasing substantially identical securities** within 30 days of the loss sales (wash sale rule).
5. **Document the harvested losses** for tax reporting and track any carryforward amounts.

## Important Caveats

- **This is not tax or investment advice.** Consult a qualified tax professional or financial advisor before executing trades in a taxable account.
- **Transaction costs and bid-ask spreads** should be considered, though these are typically minimal for broadly traded ETFs and mutual funds.
- **State taxes** may apply in addition to federal capital gains taxes.
- **If you also hold similar positions in tax-advantaged accounts** (IRA, 401(k)), purchases in those accounts of substantially identical securities can also trigger wash sale disqualification.
- **Consider whether the portfolio will naturally drift back** due to expected contributions or withdrawals before executing trades.
