# Compliance Requirements for Opening a Family Trust Account with Mixed Jurisdictions

## 1. Customer Identification Program (CIP) / Know Your Customer (KYC)

Under the USA PATRIOT Act and FinCEN's CIP rule (31 CFR 1020.220), you must identify and verify the identity of the trust and each individual associated with it.

### The Trust Entity Itself
- Obtain the full legal name of the trust, date of establishment, and jurisdiction of formation.
- Obtain the trust's Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN/EIN). If the trust does not have one, it must apply for an EIN with the IRS before account opening.
- Obtain a complete copy of the trust agreement (or a trust certification/abstract) to confirm the trust's existence, the identity of trustees, beneficiaries, and the powers granted.

### Individual Trustees (the two US persons)
- Collect full legal name, date of birth, residential address, and Social Security Number (SSN) for each.
- Verify identity using government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport) and cross-reference against reliable databases.
- Screen against OFAC's Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list, FinCEN 314(a) requests, and any other applicable sanctions or watchlists.

### The UK-Resident Trustee
- Collect full legal name, date of birth, residential address, and passport number or other government-issued identification.
- Because this individual is a non-US person, additional considerations apply:
  - Verify identity using a valid passport or equivalent government-issued ID from the UK.
  - Determine whether the individual is a US tax resident or has any US tax obligations (dual citizenship, green card status, substantial presence).
  - Screen against OFAC SDN list, UK HM Treasury sanctions list, EU sanctions lists, and any other applicable jurisdictional sanctions.
  - Consider whether the individual is a Politically Exposed Person (PEP) under both US and UK standards.

### The Corporate Trustee
- Identify the corporate trustee entity: full legal name, jurisdiction of incorporation, principal place of business, and EIN or equivalent registration number.
- Obtain articles of incorporation or equivalent formation documents.
- Identify the beneficial owners of the corporate trustee who own 25% or more of equity interests, and one individual with significant management responsibility (per FinCEN's Beneficial Ownership Rule, 31 CFR 1010.230).
- Verify the corporate trustee's registration and good standing.
- Screen the entity and its beneficial owners/controlling persons against OFAC, FinCEN, and other sanctions lists.

## 2. Beneficial Ownership (CDD Rule)

Under FinCEN's Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Rule (effective May 2018, updated with the Corporate Transparency Act requirements):

- For the trust itself, identify all individuals who directly or indirectly own 25% or more of the beneficial interests of the trust (this includes beneficiaries, not just trustees).
- Identify at least one individual who has significant responsibility for managing the trust (typically the lead trustee or the corporate trustee's authorized representative).
- For the corporate trustee, separately identify its beneficial owners under the same thresholds.
- Collect and verify name, date of birth, address, and SSN/passport number for each identified beneficial owner.

## 3. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Due Diligence

- Assign a risk rating to the account. A multi-jurisdictional trust with a corporate trustee and a foreign-resident individual trustee will likely warrant Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) rather than standard due diligence.
- Document the source of funds and expected account activity.
- Establish the purpose of the account and the nature of the trust's activities.
- For the UK-resident trustee, assess whether their involvement creates any higher-risk indicators (cross-border transactions, complex ownership structures).
- File a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) if any red flags are identified during onboarding.

## 4. OFAC and Sanctions Screening

- Screen all parties (the trust entity, all three individual trustees, the corporate trustee entity, its beneficial owners, and named beneficiaries) against:
  - OFAC SDN and Consolidated Sanctions Lists
  - Sectoral Sanctions Identifications (SSI) List
  - UK HM Treasury Consolidated List (given the UK-resident trustee)
  - Any other applicable sanctions regimes
- Screening must be performed at account opening and on an ongoing basis.

## 5. Tax Compliance and Reporting

### FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act)
- Determine the trust's FATCA classification. A US trust with US trustees will generally be a US person for FATCA purposes, but the presence of a UK-resident trustee may complicate the analysis.
- Obtain a W-9 from each US person trustee and from the trust itself if it has a US TIN.
- Obtain a W-8BEN or W-8BEN-E from the UK-resident trustee as appropriate.
- Determine whether the trust is a Foreign Financial Institution (FFI) or a Non-Financial Foreign Entity (NFFE) for FATCA purposes. Most family trusts are NFFEs.

### CRS (Common Reporting Standard)
- If your firm has obligations under CRS (as a financial institution in a CRS-participating jurisdiction), determine the trust's CRS classification and reporting obligations.
- The UK-resident trustee creates a reporting nexus to UK HMRC under CRS.

### IRS Reporting
- Determine whether the trust is a grantor trust, simple trust, or complex trust for US tax purposes, as this affects reporting (Form 1041, Schedule K-1).
- If the trust has foreign trustees or foreign beneficiaries, consider whether Form 3520/3520-A reporting obligations apply.

## 6. Suitability and Fiduciary Considerations

- If the account will be managed on a discretionary basis, ensure an Investment Policy Statement (IPS) is established that reflects the trust's objectives, risk tolerance, time horizon, and any constraints from the trust document.
- Under FINRA Rule 2111 (Suitability) and Reg BI (if applicable), document that recommended investments are suitable for the trust's stated objectives.
- Review the trust document for any investment restrictions, permitted asset classes, or concentration limits imposed by the settlor.
- Confirm which trustees have authority to direct investment decisions and whether the corporate trustee has delegated or shared investment authority.

## 7. Authorization and Documentation

- Obtain a board resolution or equivalent authorization from the corporate trustee confirming who is authorized to act on behalf of the trust.
- Confirm that the trust agreement permits the opening of the account and the type of trading/investment activity anticipated.
- Obtain signature cards or authorized signer documentation for all parties who will have trading or withdrawal authority.
- Determine whether all three trustees must act jointly or whether any subset can act independently (joint vs. several authority).

## 8. Ongoing Monitoring

- Establish a schedule for periodic review of the account's risk profile and KYC information.
- Monitor transactions for unusual or suspicious activity consistent with the risk profile.
- Re-screen all parties against sanctions lists on a regular basis and upon trigger events (e.g., a trustee change, address change, or change in beneficial ownership).
- Update CIP/CDD information if any trustee changes, beneficiaries are added, or the trust is amended.

## 9. Cross-Border Regulatory Considerations

- If the firm is also regulated in the UK (or has a UK affiliate), ensure compliance with UK FCA rules regarding client categorization and the conduct of business.
- Consider whether the UK-resident trustee's involvement triggers any requirement to register or notify the FCA.
- Assess whether marketing or solicitation rules apply in the UK if the trustee is being actively engaged from the UK.
- Evaluate whether data privacy regulations (GDPR in the UK/EU, state privacy laws in the US) impose requirements on the handling of personal information collected from the trustees.

## Summary Checklist

| Requirement | Key Actions |
|---|---|
| CIP/KYC | Identify and verify all trustees (individual and corporate), collect trust documentation |
| Beneficial Ownership | Identify 25%+ beneficial owners and one controlling person for both the trust and the corporate trustee |
| AML/EDD | Assign elevated risk rating, document source of funds, establish account purpose |
| Sanctions Screening | Screen all parties against OFAC, HM Treasury, and other applicable lists |
| FATCA/CRS | Classify the trust, collect W-9/W-8 forms, determine reporting obligations |
| Tax Reporting | Determine trust type (grantor/simple/complex), assess Form 3520 obligations |
| Suitability | Establish IPS, document suitability, review trust document constraints |
| Authorization | Confirm signing authority, joint/several powers, corporate resolutions |
| Ongoing Monitoring | Periodic KYC refresh, transaction monitoring, sanctions rescreening |
| Cross-Border | Assess UK regulatory requirements, data privacy obligations |
