# Redesigning Your Account Opening Workflow to Reduce NIGO Rate

A 28% NIGO (Not In Good Order) rate is roughly triple the industry target. The three rejection categories you identified -- missing signatures, incorrect entity titling, and incomplete beneficiary designations -- are all preventable with process redesign. Here is a structured approach to get below 10%.

---

## 1. Root Cause Analysis of Your Top Three Rejection Categories

### Missing Signatures
- Paper forms with multiple signature blocks are easy to skip.
- Clients sign in one place and assume they are done.
- Wet-signature requirements on supplemental forms (e.g., margin agreements, options applications) are often overlooked.

### Incorrect Entity Titling
- Advisors or staff manually key entity names that do not match the trust document, articles of incorporation, or partnership agreement exactly.
- Common errors: abbreviating "Trust" vs. "Revocable Living Trust," omitting trustee names, wrong date-of-trust, using a DBA instead of the legal entity name.

### Incomplete Beneficiary Designations
- Beneficiary sections are treated as optional by staff or clients.
- Per-stirpes vs. per-capita elections are left blank.
- Contingent beneficiaries are skipped entirely.
- Percentages do not sum to 100%.

---

## 2. Redesigned Workflow: Four-Stage Gate Process

### Stage 1: Pre-Fill and Data Gathering (Before the Client Meeting)

- **Collect governing documents up front.** For entity accounts (trusts, LLCs, corporations), require the trust certificate, articles of organization, or operating agreement before any forms are generated. Extract the exact legal title, formation date, and authorized signers from these documents.
- **Use a structured intake form** (digital preferred) that forces required fields: account type, exact legal title, SSN/TIN, all beneficial owners, and beneficiary designations with percentage allocations.
- **Pre-populate forms** from the intake data. Never have advisors or clients fill account opening paperwork from a blank form. Schwab's ACAT and new account forms accept pre-filled data -- use it.

### Stage 2: Internal Quality Review (Before Client Signs)

- **Dedicated reviewer.** Assign a trained operations associate (not the advisor) to review every application before it goes to the client for signature. This single step alone typically cuts NIGO rates by 40-60%.
- **Use a checklist specific to account type.** Different account types have different NIGO risks:

| Account Type | Key Check Items |
|---|---|
| Individual/Joint | Signature(s), beneficiary designation, TOD elections |
| Revocable Trust | Title matches trust certificate exactly, trustee names, trust date, certification of trust attached |
| Irrevocable Trust | Same as revocable plus EIN (not SSN), authorized party verification |
| LLC/Corp | Title matches articles exactly, EIN, authorized signer resolution, operating agreement |
| IRA/Roth IRA | Beneficiary primary + contingent, percentages sum to 100%, per-stirpes/per-capita elected |
| UGMA/UTMA | Custodian named, minor's SSN, state of residency |

- **Flag missing signatures proactively.** Mark every signature line on the pre-filled forms with a visible tab or highlight before sending to the client. Count the required signatures and note the count on the checklist.

### Stage 3: Client Signing Session (Controlled Execution)

- **Prefer e-signature where Schwab supports it.** Schwab's electronic account opening flow enforces required fields and will not let a submission proceed with missing signatures or blank required sections. If your current process is paper-heavy, migrating to e-signature is the single highest-impact change you can make.
- **If paper is required:** Use signature tabs (physical flags on the pages) and walk the client through every signature block. Have the advisor or assistant verify completion before the client leaves.
- **For entity accounts:** Have the client confirm the legal title verbally during the signing session. Read it back from the form.

### Stage 4: Final QC Before Submission to Schwab

- **Second-set-of-eyes review.** A different person from Stage 2 (or the same person on a different day) does a final check against the NIGO checklist before submission.
- **Schwab-specific validation:** Check for common Schwab-specific rejections:
  - Account title character limits
  - Beneficiary SSN requirements
  - Missing W-9 or W-8BEN for applicable accounts
  - Margin/options suitability alignment with client profile
- **Do not batch submissions.** Submit accounts as they clear QC rather than accumulating a weekly batch. Batching delays feedback and makes it harder to identify systemic errors.

## 3. Tracking and Continuous Improvement

### NIGO Dashboard
Track every submission with the following fields:
- Date submitted
- Account type
- Advisor name
- Rejection (yes/no)
- Rejection reason (use Schwab's exact rejection codes)
- Days to resolve

### Weekly Review
- Review all NIGOs from the prior week in a 15-minute operations huddle.
- Identify whether the failure was a process failure (checklist missed it) or a training failure (staff did not know the requirement).
- If a single rejection reason exceeds 5% of submissions for two consecutive weeks, update the checklist or add a specific validation step.

### Advisor Scorecards
- Share NIGO rates by advisor monthly. Advisors with persistently high rates need targeted coaching. Often the issue is that certain advisors skip the pre-fill stage and submit handwritten forms.

## 4. Quick Wins to Implement This Week

1. **Create the account-type-specific checklists** described in Stage 2. Even without any other process change, a checklist review before submission will cut your NIGO rate significantly.
2. **Require governing documents before form generation** for all entity accounts. This eliminates the most common titling errors.
3. **Mandate e-signature** for all account types where Schwab supports it. This eliminates missing-signature rejections for those account types entirely.
4. **Add a beneficiary completeness check** to your checklist: primary beneficiaries designated, contingent beneficiaries designated, percentages sum to 100%, per-stirpes/per-capita election made.
