# 401(k) to IRA Rollover: Standard of Care and Documentation Requirements for a Dual-Registrant

## Which Standard Applies

Because you are a dual-registrant, the standard of care depends on **the capacity in which you are making the rollover recommendation**, not your firm's registration status overall.

- **If you are making this recommendation in your broker-dealer capacity:** SEC Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI, 17 CFR 240.15l-1) applies. Reg BI is a point-of-recommendation standard requiring you to act in the client's best interest at the time of the recommendation, but it does not impose an ongoing monitoring obligation.
- **If you are making this recommendation in your investment adviser capacity:** The full IA fiduciary duty under Advisers Act Sections 206(1) and 206(2) applies. This is a broader, ongoing standard that includes a continuous duty of care, duty of loyalty, ongoing monitoring, and best execution obligations.

**Critical point for dual-registrants:** You must clearly disclose to your client which capacity you are acting in for this rollover recommendation *before or at the time* of the recommendation. The SEC has specifically warned against "hat-switching" -- moving a customer between BD and IA capacity without clear disclosure, potentially to exploit the less demanding standard. You cannot choose the lower standard to avoid obligations.

Additionally, **if the IRA will be an advisory account**, the fiduciary standard applies to the ongoing relationship even if the rollover recommendation itself was made in a BD capacity. And regardless of which SEC/FINRA standard applies, **PTE 2020-02 (DOL Prohibited Transaction Exemption)** likely applies to this rollover because you are recommending that assets move from an ERISA-covered plan to an IRA where you will receive compensation. PTE 2020-02 requires you to act in the customer's best interest, provide balanced disclosure, charge only reasonable compensation, adopt anti-conflict policies, and conduct retrospective compliance reviews.

## What You Must Document

### 1. Capacity Disclosure
- Written disclosure of whether you are acting as a broker-dealer representative (Reg BI) or investment adviser representative (fiduciary) for this recommendation.
- Client acknowledgment of the applicable standard and what it means for the services and protections they will receive.

### 2. Form CRS Delivery
- Confirm that the client has received your firm's Form CRS, which as a dual-registrant must describe both the brokerage and advisory relationships. Form CRS must be delivered before or at the time of the recommendation.

### 3. Alternatives Analysis (Required Under Both Standards)
You must document your evaluation of all four alternatives available to this client:

| Alternative | Factors to Document |
|---|---|
| **Stay in the 401(k)** | Investment options, plan fees (administrative and fund expense ratios), available services (managed accounts, financial planning), creditor protection under ERISA |
| **Roll to a new employer's plan** | Whether the client has or anticipates new employment with a plan that accepts rollovers |
| **Roll to an IRA with your firm** | Investment options, total cost (advisory or brokerage fees plus fund expenses plus custodial fees), services provided, state-law creditor protections |
| **Take a cash distribution** | Tax consequences, 10% early withdrawal penalty analysis |

"Do nothing" (remaining in the current plan) is explicitly identified by the SEC as a reasonably available alternative that must be considered.

### 4. Factors Specific to This Client

Given the client's profile ($340K, age 58), you must specifically address and document:

- **Age 55 rule (IRC 72(t)(2)(A)(v)):** If this client separates from service at age 55 or older, they can take penalty-free withdrawals from the 401(k). Rolling to an IRA forfeits this -- IRA penalty-free withdrawals are not available until age 59-1/2. At age 58, the client is within the window where this matters: if they roll to an IRA, they face approximately 18 months during which early withdrawals would incur a 10% penalty that they would not face if the money stayed in the 401(k). Document whether the client may need access to funds before 59-1/2.
- **Fee comparison:** Obtain and document the 401(k) plan's investment options and fees (plan-level administrative fees, fund expense ratios) and compare them to the total cost in the proposed IRA (your advisory or brokerage fees, fund expense ratios, custodial fees). The SEC Staff Bulletin on Account Recommendations (2022) makes clear that this comparison must be specific and documented, not generic.
- **ERISA creditor protections:** 401(k) assets have broad federal creditor protection under ERISA. IRA protections vary by state and are generally narrower. Document whether creditor protection is a relevant consideration for this client.
- **Investment options comparison:** Document whether the IRA provides meaningfully broader or better investment options than the 401(k), and whether those options serve this client's specific needs.
- **Net Unrealized Appreciation (NUA):** If the 401(k) holds appreciated employer stock, document whether NUA treatment under IRC 402(e)(4) was considered. An in-kind distribution to a taxable account could allow long-term capital gains treatment on the appreciation; rolling the stock into an IRA forfeits NUA treatment permanently.
- **Required Minimum Distributions:** Document any differences in RMD treatment. For a still-employed participant, 401(k) plans may allow deferral of RMDs; this becomes relevant as the client approaches age 73.
- **Services and planning value:** If the IRA provides access to financial planning, ongoing advice, or managed account services not available in the 401(k), document these as part of the rationale.

### 5. Conflict of Interest Disclosure and Mitigation
- Disclose the compensation you and your firm will receive if the rollover occurs (commissions, advisory fees, 12b-1 fees, revenue sharing, custodial fee revenue).
- Disclose that you have a financial incentive to recommend the rollover because you do not earn compensation on assets remaining in the 401(k).
- Under Reg BI: this conflict must be identified, disclosed, and mitigated. Under the IA fiduciary standard: full and fair disclosure must be provided and the client must give informed consent.
- Under PTE 2020-02: compensation must be reasonable and anti-conflict policies must be in place.

### 6. Recommendation Rationale
- A written summary of why the rollover is in this specific client's best interest, considering all the factors above.
- If the IRA is more expensive than the 401(k), you must have and document a reasonable basis for why the costlier option better serves the client despite the fee differential.

## Summary Checklist

- [ ] Disclose capacity (BD or IA) in writing; obtain client acknowledgment
- [ ] Deliver Form CRS (dual-registrant version)
- [ ] Document evaluation of all four alternatives (stay in plan, roll to new employer plan, roll to IRA, cash distribution)
- [ ] Compare fees: 401(k) plan fees vs. proposed IRA total cost
- [ ] Address the age-55 penalty-free withdrawal rule (client is 58, may need access before 59-1/2)
- [ ] Evaluate NUA eligibility if employer stock is held
- [ ] Compare ERISA creditor protections vs. state IRA protections
- [ ] Compare investment options (401(k) lineup vs. IRA shelf)
- [ ] Disclose all compensation conflicts (your fees, firm revenue from the IRA)
- [ ] Document the specific rationale for why the rollover is in this client's best interest
- [ ] If PTE 2020-02 applies, ensure compliance with its conditions (best interest, reasonable compensation, anti-conflict policies, retrospective review)
