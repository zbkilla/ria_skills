# Rollover Recommendation: Standard of Care and Documentation Requirements

## Which Standard of Care Applies?

As a dual-registrant, the standard depends on the capacity in which you are acting for this specific transaction:

1. **If you are recommending the rollover in your broker-dealer capacity**, Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI) applies. Under Reg BI (effective June 30, 2020), you must act in the retail customer's best interest at the time of the recommendation, without placing your own financial interests ahead of theirs. Reg BI has four component obligations: Disclosure, Care, Conflict of Interest, and Compliance.

2. **If you are recommending the rollover in your investment adviser capacity**, the fiduciary duty under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 applies. This is a higher, ongoing duty of care and loyalty — you must act in the client's best interest at all times, not just at the point of recommendation.

3. **Critical point for rollovers specifically**: A 401(k)-to-IRA rollover recommendation is treated as a "recommendation" under Reg BI when made in a BD capacity. FINRA and the SEC have made clear that rollover recommendations are squarely within Reg BI's scope. The SEC's June 2022 Staff Bulletin on Account Recommendations specifically addresses rollovers and emphasizes the Care Obligation analysis required.

**For a $340K account with a 58-year-old client, you need to determine and document which hat you are wearing.** If the IRA will be a brokerage account, you are likely acting as a broker and Reg BI applies. If it will be an advisory (fee-based) account, you are likely acting as an adviser and fiduciary duty applies. If there is any ambiguity, the more protective standard (fiduciary) is the safer assumption.

## Care Obligation Analysis You Must Perform (Under Either Standard)

You need to evaluate whether the rollover itself is in the client's best interest by comparing the 401(k) to the proposed IRA across these factors:

- **Fees and expenses**: Compare the all-in cost of the 401(k) plan (expense ratios, recordkeeping fees, revenue sharing) against the proposed IRA (advisory fees or commissions, fund expense ratios, account fees). 401(k) plans often have access to institutional share classes with lower expense ratios.
- **Available investments**: Does the IRA offer meaningfully better or broader investment options than the 401(k)?
- **Services**: What additional services does the client gain (financial planning, tax management, consolidated reporting)?
- **Penalty-free access**: At 58, the client could potentially take penalty-free distributions from the 401(k) under the "separation from service at age 55" rule (IRC Section 72(t)(2)(A)(v)) if they have left or are leaving the employer. Rolling to an IRA would forfeit this — IRA distributions before 59 1/2 are subject to the 10% early withdrawal penalty unless another exception applies. This is a material consideration that must be documented.
- **Creditor protection**: 401(k) assets have unlimited federal creditor protection under ERISA. IRA protection varies by state.
- **Existing employer stock**: If the 401(k) holds appreciated employer stock, Net Unrealized Appreciation (NUA) treatment could be more tax-efficient than rolling it into the IRA.

## Documentation You Need to Keep

### Under Reg BI (Broker Capacity)

1. **Form CRS (Client Relationship Summary)** — must be delivered before or at the time of the recommendation if not already provided.
2. **Rollover documentation/worksheet** — most BD compliance departments require a standardized rollover analysis form that captures:
   - Reason for the rollover recommendation
   - Comparison of fees (401(k) vs. proposed IRA)
   - Comparison of investment options
   - Comparison of services
   - Consideration of alternatives (stay in plan, roll to new employer plan, Roth conversion, partial rollover)
   - Discussion of the age-55 separation rule and early withdrawal implications
   - Account type recommended and rationale (brokerage vs. advisory)
3. **Conflict of interest documentation** — disclose that you and your firm earn compensation from the IRA that you would not earn if the client stayed in the 401(k). Document how you mitigated this conflict.
4. **Client communication records** — notes from conversations, emails, any materials provided to the client.
5. **Account opening documents** — IRA application, beneficiary designation, suitability/investor profile questionnaire.

### Under Fiduciary Duty (Adviser Capacity)

All of the above, plus:

6. **Investment advisory agreement** — signed before providing advice.
7. **ADV Part 2A and 2B** — delivered before or at the time of entering the advisory relationship.
8. **Ongoing suitability documentation** — since the fiduciary duty is ongoing, not just at point of sale.

## Practical Recommendations

- **Document the age-55 rule analysis explicitly.** At 58, this is a live issue. If the client has separated or is separating from the employer, they could access the 401(k) penalty-free now but would need to wait until 59 1/2 for IRA penalty-free access. If the client does not need the money before 59 1/2, document that.
- **Document the fee comparison with specifics.** Do not use vague language. Show the actual basis-point difference and dollar impact on a $340K account.
- **Document the alternatives you considered.** Reg BI and fiduciary duty both require you to consider reasonable alternatives — staying in the plan, rolling to a new employer's plan, doing a partial rollover, or a Roth conversion.
- **Retain records for at least 6 years** (FINRA Rule 4511 / SEC Rule 17a-4 for BD records) or as required by your firm's retention policy if longer.

---

## Bottom Line

For a dual-registrant, the standard is determined by the capacity in which you act. Either way, the rollover of a $340K account for a 58-year-old demands a thorough, documented comparison of costs, services, investment options, and — critically — the early access implications of losing the age-55 rule. The regulators have made rollover recommendations a priority examination area, so robust documentation is essential.
