# Rebalancing a Drifted 60/40 Portfolio with Tax-Loss Harvesting

## Should You Rebalance?

Yes. Your equity allocation is 68%, which is 8 percentage points above your 60% target. Since your threshold band is +/- 5% (tolerating 55%-65% equities), you are 3 points beyond the upper band. This triggers a rebalance.

## How Much to Trade

- **Current allocation:** $544K stocks / $256K bonds (68/32 on $800K)
- **Target allocation:** $480K stocks / $320K bonds (60/40)
- **Required trades:** Sell approximately $64K in equities and buy $64K in bonds

## Coordinating with Tax-Loss Harvesting

Since you have approximately $15K in unrealized losses in some equity positions, you should prioritize selling those losing positions first as part of the $64K equity reduction. Here is the recommended sequencing:

### Step 1: Sell the Losing Equity Positions First

Harvest the ~$15K in unrealized losses. This gives you a tax deduction (up to $3K/year against ordinary income, with the remainder offsetting capital gains) and moves you partway toward your rebalancing target.

### Step 2: Sell Additional Equity Positions to Close the Gap

You still need to sell roughly $49K more in equities after harvesting the losers. When choosing which lots to sell, prefer positions with the smallest gains or longest holding periods to qualify for lower long-term capital gains rates. Use specific lot identification (not average cost) to control the tax impact.

### Step 3: Reinvest Proceeds into Bonds

Deploy the full ~$64K into your bond allocation to restore the 60/40 target.

### Step 4: Avoid Wash Sales

If you want to stay invested in the same equity sectors where you harvested losses, you must wait 31 days before repurchasing "substantially identical" securities. In the interim, you can buy a similar-but-not-identical fund (e.g., swap an S&P 500 index fund for a total market fund, or swap one bond ETF for another tracking a different index) to maintain your market exposure without triggering the wash sale rule.

## Net Tax Benefit

The $15K in harvested losses can offset capital gains you realize on the other $49K of equity sales, reducing your net taxable gain. For example, if the $49K in sales generates $10K in gains, the $15K in losses would eliminate that tax liability entirely and leave you with $5K in losses to carry forward (or offset $3K of ordinary income this year with $2K carried to next year).

## Key Considerations

- **Transaction costs:** Minimize the number of trades if you pay commissions, though most brokerages are now commission-free for ETFs and stocks.
- **Check all accounts for wash sales:** The wash sale rule applies across all your accounts (including IRAs), so do not repurchase substantially identical securities in any account within the 30-day window.
- **Document your cost basis method:** Ensure your brokerage is set to specific identification so you can choose which lots to sell.
- **Rebalance fully, not partially:** Since you are already outside the band, restore to the 60% target rather than just getting back inside the 65% upper limit. This reduces the frequency of future rebalancing trades.

## Summary

Rebalance now by selling $64K in equities (starting with the $15K in losing positions for tax-loss harvesting), then buying $64K in bonds. The harvested losses offset gains on the remaining equity sales, making this a tax-efficient rebalance. Use substitute securities for 31 days to avoid wash sales on the harvested positions.
