# Fee Disclosure Guidance for Form ADV Part 2A — Item 5

Your situation involves three distinct compensation streams, each with specific disclosure requirements under Form ADV Part 2A Item 5 (Fees and Compensation). Below is how to structure and disclose each one.

---

## 1. Tiered AUM Fee Schedule

Your tiered advisory fee must be presented as a clear fee schedule showing every tier, the applicable rate, and how the blended fee is calculated. A compliant disclosure would read:

> **Advisory Fee Schedule**
>
> | Assets Under Management | Annual Fee Rate |
> |---|---|
> | First $1,000,000 | 1.00% |
> | Next $2,000,000 ($1M - $3M) | 0.75% |
> | Above $3,000,000 | 0.50% |
>
> Fees are calculated on a tiered (blended) basis. For example, a $4,000,000 account would pay: (1.00% x $1,000,000) + (0.75% x $2,000,000) + (0.50% x $1,000,000) = $30,000 annually, or an effective rate of 0.75%.

Beyond the schedule itself, Item 5 requires you to disclose:

- **Billing method and frequency.** State whether the fee is billed monthly or quarterly, in advance or in arrears, and the specific day on which the fee is calculated (e.g., based on the account value on the last business day of the prior quarter).
- **Pro-ration for partial periods.** Describe how fees are calculated if an account opens or closes mid-billing period.
- **Refund policy.** If you bill in advance, explain how prepaid fees are refunded upon termination. If in arrears, state that no refund is applicable since the fee is earned before collection.
- **Minimum account size.** If you have one, disclose it. If you waive it at your discretion, say so.

## 2. Fixed Financial Planning Fee

The $2,500 annual financial planning fee must be disclosed separately from the AUM fee so clients understand it is an additional cost. Your disclosure should cover:

- **What the fee covers.** Describe the financial planning services included (comprehensive plan, specific planning modules, ongoing updates, etc.).
- **How and when it is charged.** Is it billed as a lump sum at engagement, split into installments, deducted from the investment account, or invoiced separately?
- **Whether it is required or optional.** If the planning fee is separate from and in addition to the AUM fee, state that clearly. If clients can decline planning services and pay only the AUM fee, disclose that option.
- **Refund terms.** If a client terminates before the planning period ends, describe any pro-rated refund.

A common pitfall is burying the planning fee inside the AUM fee discussion. Treat it as a distinct line item so a reader scanning Item 5 can identify every fee they will pay.

## 3. 12b-1 Fee Revenue — This Is the Critical Disclosure

Receiving 12b-1 fees from mutual fund positions is a form of third-party compensation that creates a conflict of interest. This is the area where the SEC has brought the most enforcement actions, including the Share Class Selection Disclosure Initiative (2018), which resulted in over $139 million in disgorgement from advisers who failed to properly disclose this revenue.

Your ADV Part 2A must include all of the following:

### a. Disclose That You Receive 12b-1 Fees
Do not use vague language like "some funds we recommend may charge distribution fees." Instead, state explicitly:

> [Firm name] and/or its supervised persons receive 12b-1 distribution fees from certain mutual funds held in client accounts. These fees are paid by the fund companies out of fund assets and represent additional compensation to the firm beyond the advisory fee described above.

### b. Quantify or Estimate the Amount
Provide enough specificity for a client to understand the magnitude. You can state a range (e.g., "12b-1 fees typically range from 0.10% to 0.25% of assets held in the applicable funds") or disclose the actual amounts received. The SEC expects more than a generic acknowledgment.

### c. Explain the Conflict of Interest
State plainly that receiving 12b-1 fees creates an incentive for the firm to recommend funds that pay these fees over funds that do not:

> This arrangement creates a conflict of interest because [Firm name] has a financial incentive to recommend mutual fund share classes that pay 12b-1 fees over share classes of the same fund that do not pay such fees and may have lower overall costs to the client.

### d. Disclose Your Mitigation Measures
Describe what the firm does to manage this conflict. For example:

- The firm evaluates whether lower-cost share classes (institutional, advisor, or no-load classes) are available and appropriate for each client.
- The firm credits 12b-1 fees back to client accounts (if you do this — and this is the strongest mitigation).
- The firm's investment committee reviews share class selection periodically.

### e. State the Total Cost to the Client
The SEC has emphasized that clients must be able to understand the all-in cost. Your disclosure should make clear that the total cost includes the advisory fee, the financial planning fee, AND the fund-level expenses (including 12b-1 fees). A good practice is a summary statement such as:

> In addition to the advisory fee and financial planning fee described above, clients will bear the internal expenses of the mutual funds and ETFs held in their accounts, including management fees, 12b-1 distribution fees, and other fund operating expenses. These fund-level costs are not charged by [Firm name] but reduce the investment return to the client. The total cost to the client includes [Firm name]'s advisory fee plus these fund-level expenses.

---

## Putting It All Together — Recommended Item 5 Structure

1. **Advisory fee schedule** — the tiered table with a blended fee example
2. **Financial planning fee** — amount, billing method, what is included
3. **Billing practices** — frequency, advance/arrears, pro-ration, refund policy
4. **Other fees and expenses clients will bear** — custodian fees, transaction costs, fund expense ratios, wire fees
5. **Compensation from third parties** — 12b-1 fee disclosure with conflict description and mitigation
6. **Total cost summary** — a clear statement that the client's all-in cost is the advisory fee + planning fee + fund-level expenses

---

## Additional Considerations

- **Form CRS alignment.** Your Form CRS "What are your fees?" section must be consistent with (though more summarized than) your ADV Part 2A. The 12b-1 revenue should appear as "fees paid to us by third parties" with a direction to the ADV for detail.
- **Share class documentation.** Maintain records showing you evaluated share class selection for each client holding a fund with 12b-1 fees. If an institutional class without 12b-1 fees is available and the client meets the minimum, you need a documented rationale for any recommendation of the higher-cost class.
- **Annual review.** Fee disclosures must be updated when fee schedules change, when new compensation arrangements are established, or at least annually as part of the ADV annual amendment.
