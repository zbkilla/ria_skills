# Compliance Requirements for Opening a Family Trust Account (Institutional)

## Overview

Opening an account for a family trust with mixed US/UK trustees and a corporate trustee involves multiple overlapping regulatory regimes. Below is a structured walkthrough of the key compliance requirements.

---

## 1. Entity Identification and Classification

### Trust Classification
- Determine whether the trust is a **revocable or irrevocable** trust, as this affects who the beneficial owner is under the Customer Identification Program (CIP).
- Classify the trust as a legal entity customer under FinCEN's Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Rule (31 CFR 1010.230).
- Determine whether the trust is a **simple trust** or **complex trust** for tax purposes, as this drives withholding and reporting obligations.

### Corporate Trustee
- Identify and verify the corporate trustee entity (name, address, EIN/registration number, jurisdiction of formation).
- Obtain the corporate trustee's **authorizing resolution** or board minutes confirming its authority to act on behalf of the trust.
- Identify the **natural persons** who will act on behalf of the corporate trustee (authorized signers).

---

## 2. Customer Identification Program (CIP) — USA PATRIOT Act Section 326

For each individual trustee and the corporate trustee, you must collect and verify:

### Individual Trustees (US Persons)
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address (not a P.O. Box)
- Social Security Number (SSN)
- Government-issued photo ID (verified through documentary or non-documentary methods)

### UK Resident Trustee
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Passport number or national ID, plus country of issuance
- **No SSN available** — must use passport or other government-issued identification number instead, per CIP rules for non-US persons.

### Corporate Trustee
- Full legal name of entity
- Principal place of business address
- EIN or equivalent tax identification number
- Jurisdiction of formation
- Articles of incorporation or certificate of formation (or equivalent)

---

## 3. Beneficial Ownership / CDD Rule (FinCEN — 31 CFR 1010.230)

The 2024 updated CDD rule requires identification of **beneficial owners** for legal entity customers. For a trust:

### Ownership Prong (25% or more)
- Identify any individual who owns 25% or more of the equity interests in the trust. For a family trust, this typically means each **beneficiary** holding a 25%+ interest, or the **grantor/settlor** if the trust is revocable.

### Control Prong
- Identify at least **one individual** who has significant responsibility for managing the trust. This is typically the **lead trustee** or the natural person acting on behalf of the corporate trustee.

### Required Information for Each Beneficial Owner
- Name, date of birth, address, SSN (or passport number for non-US persons)

---

## 4. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Screening

### OFAC Screening
- Screen **all trustees** (individual and corporate), **beneficial owners**, and **authorized signers** against the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list and other relevant sanctions lists.
- The UK resident trustee triggers additional screening considerations — check the **UK HM Treasury sanctions list** and **EU consolidated sanctions list** as applicable.

### Negative News / Adverse Media
- Conduct adverse media screening on all parties.

### PEP Screening
- Screen all individuals for **Politically Exposed Person (PEP)** status. A UK resident trustee may hold foreign political positions requiring enhanced scrutiny.

### 314(a) Checks
- Screen against FinCEN 314(a) lists.

---

## 5. Cross-Border / International Considerations

### FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act)
- The trust holds a US financial account. Since two trustees are US persons, the trust is likely a **US trust** for tax purposes (meeting the "court test" and "control test" under IRC Section 7701(a)(30)(E)).
- If the trust is classified as a US trust, standard US tax reporting applies (no FATCA withholding on the trust itself).
- If any analysis determines the trust is a **foreign trust** (e.g., the UK trustee's involvement shifts the control test), a **W-8BEN-E** is required, and FATCA reporting obligations under Chapter 4 apply.
- Collect **W-9** forms from US person trustees and a **W-8BEN** from the UK resident trustee for their individual capacities.

### CRS (Common Reporting Standard)
- Since one trustee is a UK tax resident, the account may trigger **CRS reporting** obligations to HMRC. Collect self-certification forms to determine the trust's tax residency for CRS purposes.

### UK Considerations
- The UK trustee may create a nexus for UK tax obligations on the trust. This is a tax advisory matter, but from an account-opening compliance perspective, document the UK trustee's tax residency status.

---

## 6. Trust Document Review

### Required Documentation
- **Certified copy of the trust agreement** (or relevant excerpts including):
  - Trust name and date of creation
  - Identification of all trustees (individual and corporate)
  - Identification of grantor/settlor
  - Identification of beneficiaries (or class of beneficiaries)
  - Powers of trustees (investment authority, distribution authority)
  - Successor trustee provisions
  - Amendment and revocation provisions
- **Certificate of Trust** (if the firm accepts this in lieu of the full trust document, per state law — e.g., under the Uniform Trust Code)
- **Corporate trustee's authorization** to open the account (board resolution or equivalent)

### Key Items to Verify
- That the trust is validly formed and currently in effect
- That the named trustees match the individuals and entity being onboarded
- That the trust document authorizes the type of account and investments contemplated
- Signature authority and whether actions require **majority, unanimous, or sole** trustee approval
- Any restrictions on investments, borrowing, or distributions

---

## 7. Suitability and Investment Profile

### Regulation Best Interest / Fiduciary Considerations
- Document the trust's **investment objectives**, **risk tolerance**, **time horizon**, and **liquidity needs**.
- Determine if the account is subject to **ERISA** (unlikely for a family trust, but confirm).
- Understand any **investment restrictions** in the trust document (e.g., no speculative investments, prudent investor standard).

### Accredited Investor / Qualified Purchaser Status
- If the trust will invest in private placements or alternative investments, determine whether it qualifies as an **accredited investor** (net assets > $5 million) or **qualified purchaser** (net investments > $5 million) under the Investment Company Act.

---

## 8. Account Documentation and Agreements

### Required Forms and Agreements
- **New Account Application** (institutional/entity version)
- **W-9** for the trust entity (if US trust) or **W-8BEN-E** (if foreign trust)
- **W-9** for each US person trustee; **W-8BEN** for the UK trustee
- **Beneficial Ownership Certification Form** (FinCEN)
- **CRS Self-Certification** form
- **Customer Agreement** / **Brokerage Agreement** signed by authorized trustees
- **Corporate Resolution** from the corporate trustee
- **Authorized Trader/Signer designation** — specify which trustees or agents can place orders and authorize transactions
- **Privacy Notice** acknowledgment (Regulation S-P)

---

## 9. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Considerations

Given the complexity of this account, consider whether EDD is warranted:

- **Cross-border element** (UK trustee) may trigger EDD under the firm's AML program.
- **Corporate trustee involvement** adds a layer of opacity requiring deeper look-through.
- **Source of funds / source of wealth** documentation — understand how the trust was funded and the origin of assets being transferred in.
- If the trust's assets exceed your firm's threshold for **high-value accounts**, apply EDD procedures accordingly.

---

## 10. Ongoing Monitoring and Reporting

### Transaction Monitoring
- Set up appropriate AML transaction monitoring for the account.
- Flag any transactions involving the UK or other foreign jurisdictions for additional review.

### SAR Filing
- Be prepared to file **Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)** if warranted.

### Periodic Review
- Schedule periodic KYC refresh (typically annually for higher-risk accounts, every 3 years for standard).
- Monitor for changes in trustee composition, beneficial ownership, or trust terms.

### Tax Reporting
- **1099 reporting** for a US trust.
- If a foreign trust, potential **1042-S** reporting for FATCA-withheld amounts.
- Consider whether **Form 3520 / 3520-A** reporting obligations arise (foreign trust with US beneficiaries or grantors).

---

## Summary Checklist

| Requirement | Key Actions |
|---|---|
| CIP | Verify identity of all 3 individual trustees + corporate trustee + authorized signers |
| CDD / Beneficial Ownership | Identify 25%+ owners and one controlling person; collect required data |
| AML Screening | OFAC, PEP, 314(a), adverse media on all parties |
| Trust Document Review | Obtain and review trust agreement; confirm authority and investment powers |
| FATCA | Determine US vs. foreign trust status; collect W-9 or W-8BEN-E |
| CRS | Self-certification for UK trustee's tax residency |
| Suitability | Document investment objectives, restrictions, accredited/QP status |
| EDD | Assess whether cross-border and structural complexity triggers enhanced review |
| Ongoing Monitoring | AML monitoring, periodic KYC refresh, tax reporting |

---

*This analysis covers the primary US compliance obligations. The firm's compliance department should review the specific facts against its own Written Supervisory Procedures (WSPs) and AML program. Legal counsel should be consulted on trust validity, cross-border tax classification, and any state-specific trust law requirements.*
